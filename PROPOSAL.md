# Title: Macroeconomic Regime-Based Sector Rotation Strategy 

# General
My idea would be to concentrate on three sectors: technological (XLK), energetical (XLE), and financial (XLF), and try to anticipate which one is the best for investors in which economic cycle. 
In other words, I aim to determine how to adjust the combination of stock holdings among these three sectors, depending on the economic circumstances. 

# Methodology
Primarily, I would use historical data and linear/nonlinear regression (depending on how I will define the relationship) to capture correlations between various economic variables and stocks in a certain sector. 
The variables that would define my macroeconomic environment would be interest rates, inflation, and market risk/volatility. Following these, I would define with a standardized (Z-scores) approach, four main economic cycles: expansion, recession, stagflation, normalization, and potentially I would additionally expand each of these four by two subcategories: high and low risk. 

Risk (volatility) would be measured by VIX, the volatility index. Then, based on the past performances and interconnections (probably for the period 2000-2025), I would try to predict the optimal allocations of stocks of these given three sectors, with maximized return and minimized risk, given the news and expected movements in inflation, monetary policy, and overall stability/instability in the stock markets.

I would use Python for coding.

# Challenge
The challenge could be finding the thresholds for economic regimes, but I believe that with standardization with Z-scores, I would be able to highly logically and correctly define economic regimes.

# Main objective
The end goal would be to get robust results and correlations between economic cycles and sector performances, therefore being able to build a generalized framework of best asset allocation due to economic conditions and to visualize clearly and intuitively how the different sectors were performing under different regimes, emphasizing the eventual pattern.

The inclusion of an additional sector, or diving into even more advanced optimization methods, may be an option if time permits.
